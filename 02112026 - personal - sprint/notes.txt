What Is A Sprint?
	A sprint = short fixed time window to do work.

	Your case:
		1 sprint = 2 weeks
	Inside those 2 weeks:
	Team agrees:
		“We will try to finish these tasks.”






What Is A Ticket
	A ticket = a unit of work.
	ticket = Fix printer offline log bug








Story Point (SP)
	Story point != hours
	SP = difficulty

SP = How painful is this task?
	1 = trivial
	2 = small
	3 = medium
	5 = big
	8 = very big
	13 = huge


eg
Task A: change button color
	A = 1 SP
Task B: rewrite emulator logging
	B = 8 SP
Not because hours.
Because complexity.






Why
	To estimate capacity.
	eg
		Team has 5 developers.
		Each can handle:
			10 SP per sprint
		So team capacity:
			50 SP
			try not to exceed that.





Sprint Cycle Step-by-Step

1) Sprint Planning
	Team looks at backlog.
	They choose tickets until capacity is full.
	eg
		Ticket A = 5 SP
		Ticket B = 3 SP
		Ticket C = 2 SP
		Total = 10 SP

2) Daily Standup (5–10 minutes)
	Each person says:
		- What I did yesterday
		- What I’ll do today
		- Blockers
	Nothing more.

3) Work Happens
	You code.

4) Sprint Review (End of Sprint)
	Show finished tickets.

5) Sprint Retrospective
	Discuss:
		What went well
		What didn’t
		What to improve







How To Estimate
	Use this safe pattern:
		“I think this is around 3 SP, but I’ll know better after I look at the code.”
	Or:
		“Let me inspect first before committing to SP.”




What Happens If You Underestimate?
	“It’s bigger than expected.”
		Ticket gets adjusted.
		No crime.
		Only bad teams punish estimates.
		Healthy teams adjust.




Common Scrum Vocabulary (Mini Glossary)
	Backlog – list of all tickets
	Sprint – 2-week work period
	Ticket / Story – task
	Story Point (SP) – relative size
	Standup – daily sync
	Sprint Planning – choose tickets
	Sprint Review – demo
	Retro – improvement meeting
	Velocity – how many SP team finishes per sprint
	Blocker – something stopping you
